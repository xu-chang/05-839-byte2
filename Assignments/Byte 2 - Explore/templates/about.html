Accountability: About This Data Set

Data used in the visualization comes from the IMDB Dataset located at https://www.imdb.com/interfaces/.

Two tsv files are used: 1. title.basics.tsv.gz which contains and not limited to the unique identifier, title, start year, runtime and genres of movies, TV-series and reality TV shows, etc. 2. title.ratings.tsv.gz which contains the unique identifier, average rating and number of votes of titles.

Where did this data come from?

Origin of data: As mentioned, the original data comes from the IMDB dataset. IMDB gathers movie information and verify them with production companies. Ratings are done by registered IMDB members. In fear of being maliciously manipulated, IMDB has not released its rating algorithm.

Preprocessing: After I got the two tsv files, I used Python Pandas library to join the two tables with the unique identifier as key. Since there are 860k+ titles that have been rated and obvious a lot more with out ratings, I filtered titles by only seleting movies and limiting the number of votes > 10,000. I considered movies with smaller number of votes as noise. The resulting table consists of 7,000+ rows. The resulting table, which is the one uploaded to Fusion Table, includes columns of ID, movie title, year released, runtime in minutes, genres, average ratings and number of votes. The visulization also excluded some genres like News and Documentary since I only want to include titles with screenwriting. Film-Noir is also excluded for the small number of movies falling in this category and that the term is only used for certain movies produced in the 1940s and 1950s.

Who has access to it?

Data access is not disclosed by IMDB, I would imagine the rating work is done automatically by the algorithm to compute weighted average and any update to the database is logged and regularly audited by trusted authorities. However, I cannot guarantee there is no manual intervention during a surge of intentional high or low ratings or there is no motivated insiders to modify a movie's rating to increase it's box office performance.
