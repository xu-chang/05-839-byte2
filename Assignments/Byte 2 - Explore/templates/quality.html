Quality: About This Data Set

Data used in the visualization comes from the IMDB Dataset located at https://www.imdb.com/interfaces/.

Two tsv files are used: 1. title.basics.tsv.gz which contains and not limited to the unique identifier, title, start year, runtime and genres of movies, TV-series and reality TV shows, etc. 2. title.ratings.tsv.gz which contains the unique identifier, average rating and the number of votes per title.

Is the data complete?

As per my filtering strategy, movies need to have more than 10,000 ratings to be selected. If the movie is this popular, it is not really possible for the worlds' biggest movie database to fail to include it. And it is also less likely for the IMDB to miss titles in the released spreadsheets. Therefore, I would consider this data complete.

Is the data coherent?

Yes, after inspection, all fields are of correct type. There is no column shift, the "genre" field has no small typos, ratings are within 0-10 range and have at most one decimal digit, no duplicate records exist. The distribution of ratings looks normal, as the figure suggests. Overall, this is a well maintained dataset.

Is the data correct?

The unknown algorithm should ensure that the ratings are immune from malicious ratings, and the ratings can be more reliable and represent the public opionion when the number of ratings is large. However, the website is in English, which means it has a bias towards the movie preferences of English-speaking audiences. Therefore, the conclusion of any study on this dataset may not be reasonably generalized for all audiences.
